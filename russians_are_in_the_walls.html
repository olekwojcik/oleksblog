I’m Not Crazy: The Russians Are in the Walls

        Something I’ve noticed when talking about Russia and its intelligence and propaganda operations is that Americans will start to side-eye me as paranoid when I say things like “Maybe the Russians are behind this…” or “I bet the Russians rigged this somehow…”

        But I’m not paranoid! Two points are important here:

1. Russian propaganda is still in Cold War mode, and part of that means that symbolic victories, such as in sports, are very important.

2. Vladimir Putin views this extended Cold War as a zero-sum game. Anything that disrupts, damages, or causes chaos to “the West” is good for him.

        One embarrassing reason why I was quite well informed on the occupation of Crimea was because it was part of Eurovision drama. In 2016, Jamala won the Eurovision Song Contest for Ukraine with the song 1944, which was about Stalin’s deportation of the Crimean Tatars. Officially, Eurovision doesn’t approve of “political” songs, but they let Jamala perform despite her song’s obvious connection to Russia’s 2014 occupation of Crimea, triggering Russian protests. She notably beat the favorite to win, Russia’s Sergey Lazarev with his song You Are the Only One. Her victory led to many Russian politicians protesting and complaining. A Russian MP said of the loss: “There is a general demonisation of Russia – that we are all evil, that our athletes are doping, that our planes violate airspace.” (Fact check: True!) The Russian government spends a lot of money to do well in the competition. When a country wins Eurovision, they host the competition the next year, and when Russia finally won in 2008, they spent more money on hosting than any country ever at the time.

In the United States, we are used to just being the best. It comes as no surprise to us Americans that we have won yet another international competition. This is not the same for Russia, who rely on more nefarious methods in order to put their competitors on top. A good example of this is the athlete Imane Khelif, who was alleged to be a transgender woman by billionaires JK Rowling and Elon Musk, among many others. Where did this rumor of Imane Khelif being either born a man or intersex come from? The International Boxing Association(IBA) claimed that she had failed a gender test and was banned from competition. The details of this gender test are unspecified, but what we do know is that the IBA is dominated by Russians and is so corrupt that it was banned from the Olympics. The IBA announced the results of this gender test and Imane Khelif’s disqualification 3 days after she had beaten the previously undefeated Russian boxing champion, Azalia Amineva, restoring Amineva’s undefeated status. Serendipitous! Russians are notorious for doping and cheating at the Olympics. I can confirm this anecdotally when it comes to international sabre fencing competitions, where I’ve been told all you can do is try your best when the referee has clearly been bribed to favor the Russian opponent.

Why do Russian MPs care about a stupid song competition? Why do Russian oligarchs interfere in international sports organizations? Because the Russian government believes in the importance of that kind of soft power. Winning international competitions is a way of legitimizing your country and showing the world, as well as yourselves, that you are a powerful and proud country. But it goes further than that: Russian propaganda wants to position Russia as opposing “The West” generally, with the invasion of Ukraine as a war not against Ukranians, but against the West. This view is self-flattering to both Putin and Russian citizens. By promoting it, Russian governments promote the idea that Russia is a worthy opponent of the West, that it is still powerful and relevant. Putin is trying to construct a narrative of Russian Empire, which includes Ivan the Terrible, Stalin, and him. In this version of the story being sold to modern-day Russians, today’s Russia is a continuation of Russian Empire, and being an empire is cool. Song competitions and sports events are just a fragment of this propaganda.

This is why I’m not crazy when I say that the Russians might be behind something! Whether it’s match fixing in the 2024 Women’s Sabre Olympics, general transphobia, or even the recent LA fires, I have precedent for what I’m saying! They’re in the walls!
